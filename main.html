What is the OECD?

The OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) is an intergovernmental organization comprising 38 member countries. These nations collaborate to discuss challenges, exchange expertise, and develop solutions for economic and social issues, with the overarching goal of fostering policies that enhance global prosperity and well-being.

What we aim to show:

This webpage presents life expectancy and potential years of life lost data from the OECD database in an accessible and intuitive format, making complex information easier to interpret.

Potential Years of Life Lost Expanded

View the values of the Years of Life lost values for individual countries and see them compared against the Life Expectancy of the country. Choose a country to view from the dropdown box below:

Examining these values reveals a strong correlation between declining years of life lost and rising life expectancy. This trend reflects advancements in healthcare, disease prevention, and treatment methods. Today, chronic illnesses like heart disease, cancer, and dementia remain leading causes of death. Improved medical interventions, early detection, and public health initiatives have contributed to longer lifespans and reduced premature mortality, highlighting the critical role of healthcare progress and accessibility in shaping global life expectancy.

Key Takeaways:

  • Data Limitations: Life expectancy data can be influenced by variations in healthcare systems, reporting inconsistencies, and national averages that may overlook regional disparities. External factors like socioeconomic conditions and policy changes also play a role.
  • Interactive Insights: Users can compare countries, analyze shifts in life expectancy, and explore correlations with years of life lost using dual-axis scaling for deeper insights.
  • Real-World Relevance: Significant global events, such as pandemics or economic crises, can cause noticeable shifts in mortality trends. Recognizing these patterns helps contextualize changes over time and understand broader societal impacts.
